# Your relevance window

This is the one file you edit. Replace the placeholders with your own. Write it once, reuse it every week. Only the signals change.

This is the only real work the synthesizer asks of you, and it decides everything downstream. A sharp window produces a sharp brief. A loose one produces noise with a score attached.

```
- Domain: [3 to 5 watch areas]
- Function: [your role, specific enough that a So What could only be true for you]
- Decision horizon: [near 0-3m / mid 3-12m / long 12m+]
```

## Worked example (delete and replace with yours)

```
- Domain: industrial automation, agentic commerce, EU AI regulation, mid-cap industrial M&A, energy procurement
- Function: CEO of a 500-person components manufacturer, asset-heavy, EU-based, selling into automotive and aerospace tiers
- Decision horizon: 3 to 12 months
```

## Tighten it after the first three briefs

- Watch areas that read like a conference agenda ("AI", "the economy") let everything through. Name the three to five topics that actually change your decisions.
- A function line a hundred people could claim produces So Whats a hundred people could claim. Add size, sector, model, constraint.
- No horizon means everything scores as urgent. The horizon is what routes long-cycle signals to Monitor instead of the brief.

Flat briefs are the diagnostic, not the failure.
