# Your relevance window

Write this once. Reuse it every week. Only the signals change.

This is the only real work the synthesizer asks of you, and it decides everything downstream. A sharp window produces a sharp brief. A loose one produces noise with a score attached.

## The template

```
- Domain: [3 to 5 watch areas]
- Function: [your role, with enough specifics that a So What
  could only be true for you]
- Decision horizon: [near 0-3m / mid 3-12m / long 12m+]
```

## Worked example

```
- Domain: industrial automation, agentic commerce, EU AI
  regulation, mid-cap industrial M&A, energy procurement
- Function: CEO of a 500-person components manufacturer, asset-
  heavy, EU-based, selling into automotive and aerospace tiers
- Decision horizon: 3 to 12 months
```

## How to tell yours is too loose

- Watch areas that read like a conference agenda ("AI", "the economy", "innovation"). Name the three to five topics that actually change your decisions.
- A function line a hundred other people could claim. Add what makes your seat specific: size, sector, model, constraint.
- No horizon, so everything scores as urgent. The horizon is what sends long-cycle signals to Monitor instead of the brief.

Tighten it after the first three briefs. The flat briefs are the diagnostic.
